Key Man Insurance

Track key person life and disability policies across Ironwood, Anchor Pro, and Peach to Palms. Protects the business if a key operator is lost.

Why Key Man Insurance

Business Continuity
If a key person dies or becomes disabled, the policy pays the business — not the family — so operations can continue, debts can be serviced, and replacements can be hired.
Lender Requirement
Hard money lenders and SBA lenders often require key man coverage on principals before funding. Having it in place speeds up approvals.
Partner Protection
In a partnership like Ironwood (Jeff + Ty), key man coverage on each partner funds the buy-sell agreement if one partner can no longer operate.
Recommended Coverage
Industry standard is 5–10× the key person's annual economic contribution to the business. For sole operators, start at $250K–$500K minimum.

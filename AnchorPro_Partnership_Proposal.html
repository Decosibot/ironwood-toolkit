BRRRR Investment Strategy

The BRRRR method is the core wealth-building engine of this partnership. Unlike flipping, which produces taxable income and requires constant deal flow, BRRRR builds an appreciating portfolio while recycling the same capital across multiple acquisitions.

The Five Phases

B

Buy — Acquire Below Market

Target distressed, off-market, or under-valued properties priced so that total cost (purchase + rehab) stays at or below 65% of After Repair Value. Anchor Pro leads acquisition, financing, and offer negotiation.

R

Rehab — Force Appreciation

Landon Renovations executes the renovation, maximizing value-add while controlling costs. Scope is defined before closing. Jeff leads all field operations and is compensated through his W-2 salary and profit split. Target: rehab complete in 60–90 days.

R

Rent — Place a Quality Tenant

Jordan coordinates tenant marketing and manages the screening process from the office. Ty handles lender and property management relations. Target: qualified tenant in place within 30 days of rehab completion. Tenant placement triggers the seasoning clock for refinance.

R

Refinance — Extract Equity

After 6–12 months seasoning, refinance at the new appraised ARV. A DSCR loan (qualifies on rental income, not personal income) is the preferred vehicle for scalability. Pull out as much capital as possible while maintaining positive cash flow.

R

Repeat — Redeploy Capital

Cash-out proceeds fund the next acquisition. The portfolio grows with the same initial capital base, compounding over time. Target 2–3 deals per year by Year 3.

Portfolio Strategy — Hold-First · Leverage for Growth · Sell When It Makes Sense

Ironwood Homes & Renovations LLC is a portfolio-first company. The default preference is to hold every property acquired — renovation, BRRRR, Peach to Palms vacation rental, veteran housing, or government program unit — and use the portfolio as collateral to build credit and fund further acquisitions. When both member companies (Anchor Pro Solutions and Landon Renovations) agree that a sale is financially advantageous, properties can be sold. Every disposition requires Unanimous Consent. The portfolio is the company's primary financial instrument: it generates monthly cash flow, appreciates over time, and serves as collateral to leverage business credit and fund further acquisitions without requiring additional capital contributions from the members.

The Core Principle — Vacancy-Proof Cash Flow

The One-Tenant-Covers-the-Note Rule

On every multifamily acquisition, the rent from a single unit must cover the full mortgage payment (PITI). This means even at 50% vacancy (one unit empty on a duplex), the property is cash-flow neutral — not a liability. At 60–80% occupancy it cash flows positively. This is the structural protection against vacancy risk at 20–40%.

The remaining partners may purchase the removed partner's economic interest at a 10% discount to appraised value as compensation for the disruption caused.

Scenario 4 — The Shotgun Clause (Last Resort)

If all other resolution methods fail and the partnership is functionally broken, either partner may invoke the Shotgun Buy-Sell Clause:

How the Shotgun Clause Works

Partner A names a price for the entire LLC. Partner B must then choose one of two options — and only has 30 days to decide:

Option 1: Buy Partner A's interest at the named price.
Option 2: Sell their own interest to Partner A at the same named price.

Because Partner A doesn't know which option Partner B will choose, they are incentivized to name a fair price. This mechanism resolves even the most contentious deadlocks quickly and fairly — with no court involvement.

Property Valuation Method

In any buyout or dissolution scenario, property values must be determined by a neutral, agreed-upon method. The following order of preference applies:

Step 2 — Single Appraisal: One licensed appraiser, cost split equally. Value is binding.

Step 3 — Two Appraisals: Each partner selects one appraiser. Final value is the average of both. Cost borne by each party for their own appraisal.

Step 4 — Three Appraisals: If the two appraisals differ by more than 10%, a third appraiser is selected by mutual agreement. The final value is the average of the two closest appraisals. Cost split equally.

New Construction — Build to Rent

New construction for rent is the next evolution of Ironwood's portfolio strategy. Where BRRRR recycles capital through distressed acquisitions, build-to-rent creates brand-new income-producing assets from the ground up — no deferred maintenance, no mystery behind the walls, and full control over the product from day one. Ironwood launches this business line through a licensed General Contractor partner while both Jeff Sarabia and Ty Jackson accumulate the supervised hours and experience required to bring the GC license in-house within 2–3 years — with two Qualifying Agents on the company's license.

Why Build to Rent

  • New construction commands 10–20% rent premium over comparable existing homes
  • Lower maintenance costs for first 5–7 years — no aged systems, no unknown repairs
  • DSCR lenders prefer new builds — clean appraisals, no condition flags
  • Full design control — build what the rental market demands (3/2, open floor plan, LVP throughout)
  • Tenants stay longer in new homes — lower turnover, lower vacancy costs

How It Fits the Capital Stack

  • Acquire land at ≤65% of finished home ARV (land + build cost combined)
  • Construction loan funds the build (typically 80% LTC)
  • DSCR refinance on completed appraised value — recycles capital
  • Holds in portfolio — monthly rent flows through standard 35/30/35 waterfall
  • Portfolio equity compounds same as BRRRR, but with newer, cleaner assets

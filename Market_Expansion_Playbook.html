The Seller Avatar
Five types, one common thread: speed and certainty over top dollar.
🏠
The Inheritor
Got the house, not the desire. Doesn't want to deal with repairs, tenants, or the market. Wants fast, fair, hassle-free.
🔑
The Tired Landlord
Problem tenants, deferred repairs, done being a landlord. Values certainty and speed over max price.
⚡
The Life Event Seller
Divorce, job loss, medical bills, relocation. Needs to move before circumstances force their hand.
👴
The Aging Homeowner
Downsizing or kids helping them sell. Trust is the primary currency. Needs hand-holding and clarity.
⏰
The Pre-Foreclosure Seller
Racing the bank. Every day matters. Needs a hero who can close in 7–10 days.
The common thread across all 5: Speed and certainty over top dollar. That's the wedge. Every outreach message, script, and offer structure should center on that promise.
The Buyer / Investor Avatar
Who buys the deals P2P finds — and who Anchor Pro and Ironwood serve on the back end.
💼
The SE Cash Buyer
Active cash buyer or HML-backed flipper in the SE market. Owns 2–10 rentals, looking to add or reposition. Wants a trusted contractor who already knows the deal.
🔗
The Vertical Buyer
Values fixed-bid scope, no surprises, and a single point of contact across P2P → Anchor Pro → Ironwood. Loyalty goes to whoever eliminates friction.
The Homeowner Avatar (Ironwood)
Direct-to-homeowner clients for Ironwood home services.
🏡
The Cobb-Equiv Homeowner
$300K–$600K home in a suburban equivalent market. Values craftsmanship and trust over lowest bid.
⭐
The Referral Engine
Refers neighbors and family when the experience is great. Responsive to before/after content and neighborhood social proof.
The vertical power move: One seller → P2P acquires → Anchor Pro scopes → Ironwood rehabs → buyer holds. Every step adds margin. Every handoff is a relationship. The avatar isn't one person — it's a chain.

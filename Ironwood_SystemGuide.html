How the Ironwood Toolkit Works Together

A plain-language guide to every tool, how data flows between them, and a step-by-step example from first lead to closed deal.

Section 1

The Big Picture

The Ironwood toolkit is a set of connected HTML tools that all live in one folder (or on GitHub Pages). Every tool is self-contained — no internet connection, no login, no subscription. All data is saved in your browser's localStorage, which means it stays on your device and is instantly available across every tool on the same browser.

Think of it like a shared whiteboard. When you add a deal in the Property Pipeline, every other tool can see it. When you fill in details in the Remodel Estimator, the same numbers can be pulled into the Profit Waterfall, the MAO Calculator, the Change Order Form, and more — without retyping anything.

Section 5

Full Example — From Lead to Close

Example Deal

123 Oak Street, Atlanta GA — Flip / Rehab

1
Ty gets a seller lead. He opens Property Pipeline and clicks "Add Deal." He enters: Address = 123 Oak St, Atlanta GA | ARV = $185,000 | Purchase Price = $95,000 | Rehab Budget = $42,000 | Deal Type = Flip | Stage = Under Contract. He saves it. The deal is now in ironwood_deals.
2
Ty checks the offer. He opens the MAO Calculator. The green Load Deal bar shows "123 Oak St, Atlanta GA · Under Contract." He selects it. ARV fills as $185,000, rehab as $42,000 automatically. The calculator confirms $78,250 is the max offer — $95K is tight but the numbers work at 65% ARV.
3
Jeff builds the estimate. He opens the Remodel Estimator, loads the deal from the green bar, and adds line items room by room — demo, framing, electrical, plumbing, flooring, kitchen, bathrooms, paint, exterior. The tool shows a $41,800 estimate. He saves it.
4
Jeff creates the scope. In the Scope of Work tool, he loads 123 Oak St and generates a written task list to share with the general contractor and any subs. It auto-fills the address and deal type.
5
Jordan sets up the client job. She opens the Client Portal, creates a new job for 123 Oak St, sets the contract value at $41,800, and creates a 3-phase payment schedule: Phase 1 (33%) = $13,794 due at contract signing · Phase 2 (33%) = $13,794 due at rough-in complete · Phase 3 (34%) = $14,212 due at project close. She saves the job. It writes to ironwood_jobs.
6
Dashboard updates instantly. The Command Center Dashboard now shows: Active Jobs +1, Contract Value +$41,800, Outstanding +$41,800. No refresh needed — it reads live from both stores.
7
Scope changes mid-project. Client wants to add a deck. Jeff opens the Change Order Form, loads 123 Oak St from the green bar — client name and address fill in automatically. He adds the deck line item ($6,200), saves the CO. New contract total = $48,000.
8
Project closes. Ty runs the Profit Waterfall. He loads 123 Oak St — purchase price, ARV, and rehab auto-fill. Final net income = $42,000 after all costs. The tool splits it: $14,700 → Landon Renovations (Jeff) · $12,600 → Anchor Pro Solutions (Ty) · $14,700 → Ironwood H&R (reinvest).
9
Print the tearsheet. For records or lender files, Ty opens the Deal Summary Tearsheet, loads 123 Oak St, and prints a clean one-page summary with the full waterfall breakdown.
10
Ty moves the deal to Closed. Back in the Property Pipeline, he drags the card to "Closed." The Dashboard updates: Exited Deals +1, Monthly Revenue +$42,000.

Section 7

Common Questions

Why doesn't my deal show up in the Load Deal dropdown?
The deal must be saved in the Property Pipeline first. Open Property Pipeline, make sure the deal has an address entered, and save it. Then refresh the tool you're working in — the dropdown will populate automatically.
Does data sync across different devices or browsers?
No — localStorage is browser-specific. Data saved in Chrome on your laptop won't appear in Safari on your phone. For now, all team members should use the same browser on the same machine, or export/import deal data using the JSON export buttons in each tool.
What happens if I clear my browser cache?
All localStorage data will be wiped. Use the Export JSON button in the Property Pipeline regularly to back up your deals. This takes 5 seconds and saves a file you can reimport anytime.
What is the 65% ARV rule?
Ironwood's max purchase price formula: (ARV × 65%) − Rehab Costs = Max Offer. This leaves enough margin for hard costs, carrying costs, and the profit split. Example: ARV $185K × 65% = $120,250 − $42K rehab = $78,250 max offer.
How does the 35/30/35 split work?
After hard costs (materials, subs, ad spend, carrying costs) and W-2 salaries are deducted, the remaining net income is split: 35% to Landon Renovations (Jeff) · 30% to Anchor Pro Solutions (Ty) · 35% reinvested into Ironwood Homes & Renovations LLC. In Phase 1, hourly rates for Jeff ($50/hr) and Ty ($30/hr) are also deducted before the split.
When do salaries kick in?
All three salaries (Jeff $5K/mo, Ty $5K/mo, Jordan $3K–$4.5K/mo) begin once the company hits $15,000/mo gross revenue for 2 consecutive months and maintains a $10,000 operating reserve. Once triggered, salaries are paid regardless of deal volume.
What is the Client Portal used for?
It's Ironwood's internal job management and client payment tool. Jordan uses the Admin view to create jobs and set payment phase schedules. Clients see a clean branded page showing their project progress and a Pay Now button (Stripe integration pending). All active job data feeds the Dashboard live.
How do I access all the tools?
Open index.html (the main hub) or the Ironwood Dashboard (Command Center). Every tool is linked from either page. You can also open tools directly from the GitHub Pages URL once it's live.

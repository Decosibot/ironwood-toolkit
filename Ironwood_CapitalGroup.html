Car Wash Strategy — Operational Edge

Car wash real estate is one of the hottest commercial asset classes in the country right now. Express tunnel washes with unlimited membership models generate predictable, recurring revenue that institutional buyers are paying premium cap rates to acquire. Ironwood has an edge most operators and investors do not: direct operational experience in the industry. That means underwriting a car wash acquisition without relying on a broker's pro forma — verifying throughput, membership penetration, equipment age, chemical costs, and labor independently.

Phase 2 — Operate Directly

  • Ironwood Car Wash LLC as subsidiary of Ironwood Capital Group
  • Target: express tunnel, membership model, high-traffic corridor
  • Site selection criteria: 30,000+ daily traffic count, 1-mile pop. density, minimal direct competition
  • Acquisition target: $800K–$2M for existing wash; greenfield build $1.5M–$3M
  • Stabilized net: $35K–$60K/mo per location at full membership ramp
  • Leverage existing operational knowledge for due diligence — no guessing

Phase 3 — NNN Lease to Operator

  • Acquire real estate only — sell or lease the operation to an established chain
  • Fully passive — tenant handles all staffing, chemicals, maintenance, marketing

  • Cap rate 6–8% on stabilized car wash real estate
  • Ironwood retains real estate appreciation — unlimited upside on land value
  • Scales without operational burden — right model at 3+ locations

Opportunity Zone Fund

The Ironwood Opportunity Zone Fund LLC raises capital from investors who have existing capital gains — real estate sales, stock sales, business exits — and deploys it into projects located in Georgia's 260 Qualified Opportunity Zone census tracts. Investors defer and potentially eliminate capital gains tax. Communities in underserved Atlanta submarkets receive quality housing and commercial development. Ironwood earns GP fees and promote.

Capital Gains DeferralDefer paying tax on the original gain until Dec 31, 2026 (or sale of OZ investment)Invest within 180 days of the gain event

Target OZ tracts: South Fulton, East Point, College Park, Vine City, English Avenue, and select tracts in Clayton County and DeKalb. Projects eligible for OZ treatment include new construction multifamily, mixed-use commercial, and veteran transitional housing. Many of these projects also qualify for Low Income Housing Tax Credits (LIHTC) — layering two separate equity sources into the same capital stack, reducing Ironwood's required equity contribution substantially.

Capital Raise Tracks

Track 1 — Reg D 506(c)

  • Investors: Accredited only ($1M+ net worth or $200K+ income)
  • Advertising: Public solicitation permitted — social media, YouTube, podcasts
  • Minimum: $5,000 per investor
  • Documents required: PPM, LPA, Subscription Agreement
  • Use case: Standard BRRRR deals, new construction, commercial acquisitions
  • Filing: Form D with SEC within 15 days of first sale

Track 2 — Reg CF Crowdfunding

  • Investors: Anyone — no accreditation required
  • Advertising: Public solicitation permitted through registered portal
  • Minimum: $100 per investor
  • Portal: Wefunder, Republic, or StartEngine (SEC-registered)
  • Use case: Veteran housing, affordable housing, community mission projects
  • Annual raise limit: Up to $5M/year

Track 1 and Track 2 are not competing — they serve different deal types and different investor audiences simultaneously. Every social media post about a veteran housing project is both a marketing asset AND a Reg CF offering promotion. The community mission and the capital strategy are the same activity.

REIT — The Long Game

When Ironwood's portfolio reaches 100+ residential doors or a substantial commercial portfolio, a Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT) election becomes a structural option. A REIT must distribute 90% of taxable income to shareholders as dividends, which eliminates corporate income tax. In exchange, it can raise equity from the general public through a public or non-traded offering structure.

1
Non-Traded REIT (Phase 3–4)
Raise capital from retail investors without going public. Minimum investment $1K–$5K. Same model as Fundrise and RealtyMogul. Opens the capital raise to tens of millions of potential investors. Realistic at $50M+ AUM.
2
UpREIT Structure
Allows Ironwood to contribute appreciated properties from subsidiary LLCs into the REIT in exchange for operating partnership units — a tax-deferred transaction. No capital gains triggered on contribution. Properties consolidate into the REIT without selling.
3
Publicly Traded REIT (Phase 4+)
Full SEC registration, quarterly earnings, public market liquidity for investors. This is the institutional end game — a 10-year horizon decision, not immediate. But every real estate acquisition made today should be made with REIT eligibility in mind.

Prerequisites — What Must Be in Place First

1
Track Record (Phase 1–2)
Document every deal from day one. Deal Summary Tearsheets showing purchase price, rehab cost, ARV, refi proceeds, cash flow, and equity. This file becomes the offering document for Fund II and institutional LPs.
2
Audited Financials
Hire a CPA firm to audit Fund I annually once the first fund vehicle closes. Audited returns are the difference between "I trust you" capital and "I've verified you" institutional capital. Budget $5K–$15K/year — it unlocks 10x that in new commitments.
3
PPM + Securities Attorney
A Private Placement Memorandum, Limited Partnership Agreement, and Subscription Agreement are required before Fund I closes to investors. Use a securities attorney experienced in real estate private placements. Budget $15K–$25K one time per fund vehicle.
4
Georgia GC License (Jeff)
Required for new construction permits, Fund the Flip financing, and institutional lender requirements. Must be in place before the first new construction fund deal closes. File through the Georgia Secretary of State — qualifying agent exam required.
5
ADV Filing at $25M AUM
Once Ironwood Capital Group manages $25M+ in assets, SEC Investment Adviser registration (Form ADV) is required. Below $25M, register with the Georgia Securities Division. Budget $5K–$10K for compliance counsel when approaching this threshold.
6
Investor Portal (20+ LPs)
Manual reporting breaks down at scale. Use Juniper Square, AppFolio Investment Management, or IMS to deliver quarterly reports, K-1s, and capital account statements automatically. Signals institutional-grade operations to LPs and retains relationships for future funds.

Risk Factors

Securities Compliance

Never accept investor capital before a PPM is in place and a securities attorney has reviewed the offering structure. Reg D and Reg CF provide exemptions from SEC registration — they are not optional guidelines. One mistake creates personal liability for the managing members.

Track Record Gap

Institutional investors will not commit $500K+ without 3–5 years of audited returns. Skipping Phase 1 to raise Fund II capital will fail. The track record is the product. Document every deal, audit every year, and the capital conversations become easy by Year 3.

Commercial Underwriting Discipline

Gas stations require Phase I environmental studies. Car washes require equipment inspection and membership data verification. Strip malls require tenant credit quality review. Never acquire commercial real estate without a commercial real estate attorney reviewing the lease and a licensed inspector on the property.

OZ Regulatory Risk

Opportunity Zone tax benefits are governed by IRS regulations created by the 2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act. Congress can modify or eliminate these benefits. All OZ fund documents must disclose this risk. Underwrite every OZ deal to work without the tax benefit — the OZ treatment is upside, not the foundation.

Scaling Ahead of Infrastructure

Moving from residential to funds to commercial before legal, compliance, and accounting infrastructure is in place creates exposure. Each phase has prerequisites. A missed step at $5M AUM is manageable. The same mistake at $50M is a legal and reputational event with LP and SEC implications.

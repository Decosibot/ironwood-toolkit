Any Member may, with the Unanimous Consent of all Members, make additional Capital Contributions to the Company. Any such additional contribution shall be reflected in an amendment to this Agreement executed by all Members, which amendment shall set forth the amount contributed, the contributing Member, and any adjustment to the Members' relative Capital Accounts or Membership Interests.

6.6  Capital Calls. The Managing Member may, with Unanimous Consent of all Members, issue a capital call requiring each Member to contribute additional capital to the Company. Any capital call in excess of Ten Thousand Dollars ($10,000.00) in the aggregate within any rolling twelve (12) month period shall require Unanimous Consent as provided in Section 5.4(a). Any capital call notice shall:

  1. Be in writing and delivered to all Members at least fifteen (15) business days prior to the required contribution date;
  2. Specify the total amount to be raised, each Member's pro-rata share based on Membership Interest percentages, the purpose for which the additional capital is required, and the date by which contributions must be made; and
  3. Be accompanied by reasonable supporting documentation demonstrating the business need for the additional capital.

6.7  Failure to Contribute. If a Member fails to make any additional Capital Contribution agreed upon or required pursuant to a capital call under Section 6.6 within the time specified, such failure shall constitute a material breach of this Agreement. In such event, the non-defaulting Member shall have the right, but not the obligation, to contribute the defaulting Member's share of such additional capital. If the non-defaulting Member elects to contribute the defaulting Member's share, the Membership Interests of the Members shall be adjusted proportionately to reflect the aggregate Capital Contributions of each Member, subject to good faith negotiation between the Members. If the Members cannot agree on an adjusted Membership Interest within thirty (30) days, either Member may invoke the dispute resolution procedures of Article XIV.

6.8  Anchor Pro Operational Capital Contributions — Interest-Free Company Loans. Notwithstanding any other provision of this Article VI, Anchor Pro Solutions, Inc. may contribute operational capital to the Company from time to time as mutually agreed in writing by both Members ("Anchor Pro Operational Contributions"). The following terms govern all Anchor Pro Operational Contributions:

  • (a) Debt Obligation, Not Equity. Each Anchor Pro Operational Contribution shall be recorded on the Company's books as a liability of the Company and a receivable of Anchor Pro Solutions, Inc. No Anchor Pro Operational Contribution shall alter, adjust, or otherwise affect the fifty percent (50%) / fifty percent (50%) Membership Interest split between Anchor Pro Solutions and Landon Renovations. Capital contributions made under this Section are debt obligations of the Company — not equity contributions — and confer no additional ownership rights upon Anchor Pro Solutions.
  • (b) Zero Interest. Anchor Pro Operational Contributions shall bear interest at zero percent (0%) per annum. No interest, return, or premium of any kind shall accrue or be owed to Anchor Pro Solutions on any Anchor Pro Operational Contribution.
  • (c) Repayment Period. Each Anchor Pro Operational Contribution shall be repaid to Anchor Pro Solutions, Inc. in full within twelve (12) months of the date the contribution was made, as recorded in the Capital Contribution Log described in Section 6.8(f). Article XVI — Subsidiary LLC StructureEvery branch of Ironwood operates as its own subsidiary LLC
Plain Language Summary — Article XVI Every distinct business line or brand operated under the Ironwood umbrella is formed as its own subsidiary LLC. Ironwood Homes and Renovations (the parent) holds the membership interests in each subsidiary. This structure provides liability isolation between divisions, clean financial separation, and a clear path to sell, partner, or scale any branch independently. Both members must agree before forming any subsidiary. Each subsidiary follows the same 50/50 ownership and fee structure as the parent unless a separate written agreement says otherwise.

16.1  Subsidiary LLC Requirement — All Branches. Each distinct business line, brand, or division operating under the Ironwood umbrella shall be organized and operated as a separate subsidiary limited liability company (each a "Subsidiary LLC"), with Ironwood LLC holding one hundred percent (100%) of the membership interests unless otherwise agreed by Unanimous Consent. No business line or brand shall be operated as an unincorporated division of Ironwood LLC once it has generated its first dollar of revenue or entered into its first contract — whichever occurs first. The following subsidiary LLCs are expressly contemplated by this Agreement and shall be formed upon Unanimous Consent at the appropriate phase of Company growth:

  2.   3. Ironwood Veteran Housing LLC — acquisition, renovation, and operation of housing units for United States military veterans and their families, including VA-backed programs, transitional housing, and veteran-preference Section 8 tenancies, as further described in Section 8.11(a);
  4. Ironwood Affordable Housing LLC — participation in federal, state, and local government-sponsored housing programs including Section 8 HCV, HUD HOME, LIHTC, and Georgia DCA programs, as further described in Section 8.11(b);
  5. Ironwood Services LLC — ancillary service businesses including without limitation dumpster rental, debris hauling, residential and commercial cleaning, property staging, and lawn care and landscaping services;
  6. Any additional Subsidiary LLC approved by Unanimous Consent for any other business line that is ancillary to, synergistic with, or complementary to the Company's primary renovation, rental, and real estate investment operations.

16.2  Formation Process. Each Subsidiary LLC shall be formed by filing Articles of Organization with the Georgia Secretary of State (or the applicable state), obtaining a separate EIN, opening a dedicated business bank account, and executing a standalone Operating Agreement specific to that subsidiary. Formation of any Subsidiary LLC requires Unanimous Consent documented by a written resolution signed by both Members prior to filing. No Member may act on behalf of a Subsidiary LLC before it has been properly formed and authorized.

16.3  Subsidiary Governance. Each Subsidiary LLC shall be governed by its own operating agreement. Unless a separate written instrument executed by both Members expressly provides otherwise, each Subsidiary LLC's governing documents shall reflect: (i) one hundred percent (100%) membership interest held by Ironwood LLC; (ii) the same fifty percent (50%) / fifty percent (50%) economic interest as between Anchor Pro Solutions and Landon Renovations flowing through Ironwood LLC's waterfall; and (iii) the same marketing and operations fee structure described in Article VIII, appropriately adapted to the subsidiary's revenue base.

16.4  Financial Separation — No Commingling. Each Subsidiary LLC shall maintain its own books of account, bank accounts, financial statements, and tax filings entirely separate from Ironwood LLC and from every other Subsidiary LLC. Revenue, expenses, and assets of each subsidiary shall never be commingled with the parent or with any sibling subsidiary. The Managing Member shall maintain clear, complete records of each subsidiary's financial performance and shall provide both Members with monthly summaries of each active subsidiary's revenue and expenses.

16.5  Revenue Flow to Parent. Each Subsidiary LLC shall distribute its net distributable profit to Ironwood LLC (as the sole member) on a schedule agreed upon in that subsidiary's operating agreement, and no less frequently than quarterly. Ironwood LLC shall then distribute its share of subsidiary profits to the Members through the standard waterfall established in Article VIII. Subsidiary revenues are not directly distributed to individual Members — all distributions flow upward through Ironwood LLC first.

16.6  Shared Marketing and Operations. Subsidiary LLCs shall share the marketing and operational infrastructure provided by Jordan Sarabia and Ty Jackson as described in Section 8.3, unless a separate written agreement provides otherwise for a specific subsidiary. Jordan Sarabia and Ty Jackson shall each be entitled to a marketing and operations fee on subsidiary revenues consistent with the fee structure in Section 8.3, unless the Members agree in writing to a different fee structure for a specific subsidiary.

16.7  Liability Isolation. The Subsidiary LLC structure is intended to isolate liability between business lines so that claims, debts, or obligations arising from one subsidiary do not attach to Ironwood LLC or to any other subsidiary. Each Subsidiary LLC shall obtain and maintain its own insurance appropriate to its operations. No Member shall personally guarantee the debts of any Subsidiary LLC without the prior written consent of both Members.

16.8  Disposition of a Subsidiary. Neither Member may sell, transfer, encumber, or otherwise dispose of Ironwood LLC's membership interest in any Subsidiary LLC, in whole or in part, without the prior Unanimous Consent of both Members. Any disposition of a Subsidiary LLC approved by both Members shall be documented by a written purchase agreement, and the net proceeds shall flow through the Ironwood LLC waterfall as Company revenue.

Article XVII — Capital Raise, Passive Investors, and CrowdfundingHow Ironwood brings in outside capital while keeping full operational control
Plain Language Summary — Article XVII This article governs how Ironwood raises outside capital. Investors come in through deal-specific Project LLCs — never directly into Ironwood LLC itself — so they have no voting rights and no control over how the Company operates. Ironwood manages everything and earns a management fee plus a share of profits above the investor's preferred return. For community-focused projects (veteran housing, affordable housing, neighborhood revitalization), the Company may raise capital through SEC-registered crowdfunding portals, allowing anyone — not just wealthy investors — to participate. In all cases, Ironwood controls the deal from start to finish.

17.1  Capital Raise Authority. Subject to the requirements of this Article XVII, Ironwood LLC (the "Sponsor") is expressly authorized to raise outside capital from passive investors for specific real estate projects and community initiatives. All capital raises shall be conducted through one of the two approved structures described in this Article and in compliance with applicable federal and state securities laws.

17.2  Project LLC Structure — No Direct Investment in Ironwood LLC. Under no circumstances shall outside investors acquire any direct membership interest in Ironwood LLC. All passive investment capital shall be raised through a separate, deal-specific limited liability company (each, a "Project LLC") formed by Ironwood LLC for that specific project. Each Project LLC shall have the following structure:

  1. Managing Member. Ironwood LLC shall serve as the sole Managing Member of each Project LLC and shall retain one hundred percent (100%) of management authority and voting rights. Ironwood LLC shall make all operational, financial, and disposition decisions for each Project LLC without consent of passive investors, except as expressly required by Section 17.5.
  2. Non-Managing Members. Passive investors shall be admitted as Non-Managing Members of the applicable Project LLC. Non-Managing Members shall have no voting rights, no management authority, no right to participate in day-to-day operations, and no right to direct or veto any decision of the Managing Member, except as provided in Section 17.5(d).
  3. Separate Operating Agreement. Each Project LLC shall be governed by its own operating agreement, consistent with the terms of this Article XVII, to be approved by both Members of Ironwood LLC prior to any investor admission.
  4. Naming Convention. Project LLCs shall be named descriptively (e.g., "Ironwood Capital Partners I LLC," "Ironwood Community Fund I LLC") and shall be wholly owned and managed by Ironwood LLC as Managing Member.

17.3  Track 1 — Accredited Investor Raises (Regulation D, Rule 506(c)). For standard investment deals — BRRRR acquisitions, flips, and income-producing renovations — the Company may raise capital from accredited investors under SEC Regulation D, Rule 506(c), subject to the following:

  1. Investor Qualification. All investors admitted under this track must be verified accredited investors as defined under Rule 501 of Regulation D (generally: individual income of $200,000+/year or net worth of $1,000,000+ excluding primary residence, or applicable entity thresholds). The Company shall obtain and retain written verification of accredited status from each investor prior to accepting any capital commitment.
  2. Public Solicitation Permitted. Rule 506(c) permits general solicitation and public advertising, including through social media platforms (Instagram, Facebook, YouTube, TikTok), the Company's website, and any other public channel. The Company and its Members may publicly discuss investment opportunities, deal performance, and return projections, provided all statements are truthful, non-misleading, and accompanied by appropriate risk disclosures.
  3. Private Placement Memorandum. Prior to admitting any investor under this track, the Company shall prepare or cause to be prepared a Private Placement Memorandum (PPM) for each offering, reviewed by a licensed Georgia securities attorney. The PPM shall disclose all material risks, the compensation structure, the projected return, and the terms of the applicable Project LLC operating agreement.
  4. Minimum Investment. Unless otherwise approved by both Members, the minimum investment per accredited investor per Project LLC shall be Five Thousand Dollars ($5,000.00).
  5. SEC Filing. The Company shall file a Form D with the SEC within fifteen (15) days of the first sale of securities in each offering under this track.

17.4  Track 2 — Community Crowdfunding (Regulation Crowdfunding / Reg CF). For community-focused and mission-driven projects — including veteran housing, affordable housing, neighborhood revitalization, and similar give-back initiatives — the Company may raise capital from the general public through SEC-registered crowdfunding portals under Regulation Crowdfunding (17 CFR Part 227), subject to the following:

  1. Eligible Projects. Track 2 raises shall be limited to projects that have a demonstrable community benefit component, including but not limited to: (i) properties to be developed under Ironwood Veteran Housing LLC; (ii) properties to be developed under Ironwood Affordable Housing LLC; (iii) neighborhood revitalization projects in underserved Atlanta Metro communities; and (iv) other community-focused projects approved by Unanimous Consent of both Members.
  2. Crowdfunding Portal. All Track 2 raises shall be conducted through a registered funding portal or broker-dealer registered with the SEC and FINRA (such as Wefunder, Republic, or StartEngine). The Company shall not accept Reg CF investments directly; all transactions shall flow through the registered portal.
  3. Non-Accredited Investors Permitted. Unlike Track 1, Track 2 permits investment by non-accredited investors subject to annual investment limits set by SEC rules. Minimum investments may be set as low as One Hundred Dollars ($100.00) to maximize community participation.
  4. Annual Raise Limit. The Company shall not raise more than Five Million Dollars ($5,000,000.00) in any twelve-month period across all Track 2 offerings, consistent with the Reg CF annual limit as adjusted by SEC rule.
  5. Form C Filing. The Company shall file a Form C with the SEC and provide all required disclosures through the applicable crowdfunding portal prior to accepting any investments under this track. Ongoing reporting obligations (Form C-U, annual updates) shall be maintained for the duration of each offering.
  6. Social Media and Content Marketing. The Company and its Members may promote Track 2 offerings publicly on social media, YouTube, and other platforms, provided all promotional content complies with Reg CF communication rules and includes the required portal link and risk disclosure language. Community crowdfunding raises may be used as content to build brand awareness, demonstrate mission alignment, and grow the Company's audience, provided marketing and securities solicitation are clearly distinguished.
  7. Community Give-Back Option. By Unanimous Consent of both Members, Ironwood LLC may elect to donate its promote or carry on any Track 2 deal back to the community served by that project — either through a nonprofit partner, a rent reduction for tenants, or reinvestment into the next community project. This election shall be documented in the applicable Project LLC operating agreement.

17.5  Investor Waterfall and Return Structure. All Project LLCs shall distribute capital and returns to investors in the following order of priority:

  1. Return of Capital. Investors receive one hundred percent (100%) of their contributed capital before any profit is distributed;
  2. Preferred Return. Investors receive a preferred return on their contributed capital, at a rate to be specified per offering but generally in the range of eight percent (8%) to ten percent (10%) per annum, accruing from the date of investment;
  3. Ironwood Promote / Carried Interest. After investors receive their capital and preferred return, remaining profits are split between investors and Ironwood LLC at a ratio specified per offering — generally seventy percent (70%) to investors and thirty percent (30%) to Ironwood LLC as the promote / carried interest (the "Promote"). The Promote compensates Ironwood LLC for deal sourcing, management, renovation, and disposition work;
  4. Investor Consent Rights (Limited). Notwithstanding the Managing Member's exclusive operational authority, Non-Managing Members shall have the right to approve or reject: (i) a sale of the Project LLC property at a price below the investors' contributed capital; and (ii) a capital call requiring additional investment from existing investors. All other decisions remain with Ironwood LLC as Managing Member.

The Promote received by Ironwood LLC flows into the Ironwood LLC operating account as Company revenue and distributes to the Members fifty percent (50%) to Anchor Pro Solutions and fifty percent (50%) to Landon Renovations through the standard waterfall.

17.6  Ironwood LLC Management Fee on Project LLCs. Ironwood LLC shall charge each Project LLC a management fee of one percent (1%) to two percent (2%) of total capital raised, or five percent (5%) of Gross Project Revenue, as specified in the applicable Project LLC operating agreement. This fee compensates Ironwood LLC for project oversight, lender coordination, construction management, and investor reporting and is paid to Ironwood LLC as a project-level expense before computing distributable profits.

17.7  Member Approval Required. Before initiating any capital raise under this Article XVII — whether Track 1 or Track 2 — the following conditions must be satisfied:

  1. Both Members must approve the specific project, the capital raise amount, the investor terms, and the Project LLC operating agreement by Unanimous Consent;
  2. A licensed Georgia securities attorney must review and approve the offering documents (PPM for Track 1; Form C and portal disclosures for Track 2);
  3. The Company must have completed at least one (1) full deal cycle (acquisition through disposition or stabilization) before initiating its first Track 1 or Track 2 offering, unless both Members unanimously agree to waive this requirement; and
  4. All required SEC and state securities filings must be completed prior to accepting any investor capital.

17.8  No Personal Guarantees by Members. Neither Member shall personally guarantee any obligation of any Project LLC, nor shall either Member pledge any personal assets as security for any Project LLC obligation, without the prior written consent of both Members. Ironwood LLC's exposure to each Project LLC is limited to its contributed capital and management obligations under the applicable Project LLC operating agreement.

17.9  Investor Reporting. Ironwood LLC shall provide each Project LLC investor with: (a) quarterly updates on project status, expenditures, and projected timeline; (b) annual financial statements for the applicable Project LLC; and (c) prompt written notice of any material change to the project scope, budget, or timeline. For Track 2 offerings, all reporting obligations required by Reg CF and the applicable crowdfunding portal shall be satisfied on time.

17.10  Securities Law Compliance. The Company and its Members acknowledge that the offer and sale of membership interests in any Project LLC constitutes the offer and sale of securities under federal and applicable state law. Neither Member shall offer, sell, or advertise any investment opportunity on behalf of the Company or any Project LLC without first ensuring that the applicable offering is properly structured and filed under an available exemption (Reg D or Reg CF) or a registered offering. Neither Member shall make any materially false or misleading statement in connection with any offering. The Company shall engage qualified legal counsel for every offering.

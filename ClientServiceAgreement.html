  1. Deposit: A non-refundable deposit is due upon signing this Agreement. Work will not commence until deposit is received.
  2. Progress Payments: If applicable, progress payments are due as outlined in the attached Scope. Contractor may pause work if a scheduled progress payment is 5+ days past due.

  4. Late Payments: Balances unpaid 10 days past due accrue interest at 1.5% per month (18% APR) and may trigger a mechanic's lien filing.

Section 4 — Change Orders

Any modification to the Scope, schedule, or price — including unforeseen conditions discovered during demolition — requires a written Change Order signed by both parties before additional work proceeds. Verbal approvals are not binding. Unforeseen structural, mechanical, electrical, or plumbing issues discovered after demo may result in a Change Order to adjust scope and price; Contractor will notify Client in writing within 2 business days of discovery.

Section 5 — Schedule & Delays

Completion dates are estimates. Contractor is not liable for delays caused by: material lead times, weather, permit timelines, subcontractor availability, or Client-initiated change orders. Client-caused delays (late selections, restricted access, late payments) may extend the schedule at Contractor's discretion and may incur additional costs.

Section 6 — Workmanship Warranty

Contractor warrants all labor for from the date of substantial completion. Warranty covers defects in workmanship only. Manufacturer warranties on materials and fixtures pass through to Client as provided by the manufacturer. Warranty is void if Client or third parties perform work on warranted items without Contractor approval.

Section 7 — Lien Rights Notice

NOTICE TO OWNER: Under Georgia law (O.C.G.A. § 44-14-360 et seq.), any contractor, subcontractor, laborer, or supplier who furnishes labor, services, or materials for improvements to real property and is not paid may have a valid lien against your property. A lien waiver will be provided upon final payment in full.

Section 8 — Insurance & Liability

Contractor carries general liability insurance. Client is responsible for maintaining homeowner's insurance on the property during the project. Contractor is not responsible for pre-existing conditions, damage from Client's failure to maintain utilities, or losses due to Client-provided materials or Owner-furnished items. Contractor's total liability shall not exceed the total contract price.

Section 9 — Dispute Resolution

The parties agree to attempt good-faith resolution of any dispute within 15 days of written notice. If unresolved, disputes shall be submitted to binding arbitration in the county where the property is located under the rules of the American Arbitration Association. The prevailing party shall be entitled to recover reasonable attorney's fees and costs.

Section 10 — Termination

Either party may terminate this Agreement with 10 days written notice. Upon termination, Client shall pay for all work completed to date plus any non-cancellable material orders. Deposit is non-refundable. If Client terminates without cause after work begins, Client owes Contractor a termination fee equal to 15% of the remaining contract balance.

Section 11 — Entire Agreement

This Agreement, together with the attached Scope of Work, constitutes the entire agreement between the parties. It supersedes all prior discussions, proposals, and representations. Amendments must be in writing and signed by both parties. This Agreement is governed by the laws of the State of Georgia.

Strategy 01
Renovation & Remodel Business
How Ironwood sources homeowner clients, prices jobs, manages execution, and converts every project into the next opportunity.

Overview

The renovation and remodeling business is Ironwood's primary cash flow engine in Phase 1. It serves two purposes simultaneously: generating operating capital to fund the BRRRR pipeline, and building the trade relationships, subcontractor network, supplier accounts, and reputation that reduce costs and increase margins on every subsequent project. Every completed renovation is a proof of execution — a before-and-after story that becomes a marketing asset, a referral source, and a demonstration of Ironwood's ability to deliver.

Jeff Sarabia leads all field execution. Ty Jackson manages deal sourcing, client acquisition, pricing, and project oversight. Jordan Sarabia handles marketing, client communications, and brand. Together, the three roles cover the full project lifecycle from lead to completion without relying on outside management.

Core Thesis

Every remodel job funds the next BRRRR deal. The renovation business is not the destination — it is the engine that builds the capital, credit, and credibility to acquire and hold real estate forever.

Lead Generation

Primary Channels

Ironwood targets homeowners who need renovation work but do not have the time, knowledge, or contractor relationships to manage it themselves. The primary lead sources are social media content, Google Business Profile, yard signs on active job sites, and direct referrals from past clients and real estate agents.

  • Social media (Instagram, Facebook, YouTube): Before-and-after content on every completed job. Jordan produces content and Ty manages paid promotion. Video walkthroughs outperform static photos — always capture video on every job site.
  • Google Business Profile: Every completed job should generate a Google review request. A 4.8+ star rating with 20+ reviews is the single most effective local lead generator for residential contractors.
  • Job site yard signs: Ironwood Renovations branded signs on every active job site. Neighbors see them daily. This is the lowest-cost lead source and consistently produces referral calls.
  • Real estate agent referrals: Agents regularly need reliable contractors for their seller clients — pre-listing renovations, post-inspection repairs, and flip projects. Build relationships with 5–10 active agents in the Atlanta metro.
  • Homeowner intake form: The Ironwood website and social media drive leads to the Homeowner Intake Form, which captures scope, budget, and timeline automatically. This pre-qualifies leads before Ty spends time on a site visit.

Pricing and Estimating

Two Client Types — Two Pricing Models

Ironwood serves two fundamentally different client types, and the pricing model must reflect that difference.

Homeowner Clients

All-inclusive fixed price. Materials, labor, markup, and profit are bundled into one client-facing number. No markup percentages are disclosed. The client sees a total project cost, a payment schedule, and a scope of work — nothing else. This protects Ironwood's margin and simplifies the client experience.

BRRRR / Internal Projects

Cost-plus model. Materials are tracked at actual cost, labor is billed at agreed rates, and markup is applied transparently between Members. The Scope of Work Tool tracks client price vs. internal cost on every line item so margin is visible at all times.

Markup Structure

Material costs carry a 20% markup on homeowner jobs by default — this covers supplier relationships, material management, and the risk of material cost fluctuations. Labor is priced based on Jeff's Phase 1 ($50/hr) or Phase 2 (30% of gross profit) structure. The Remodel Estimator tool builds the profit waterfall automatically — Ty's 3% marketing fee, Jeff's labor, Anchor Pro's 5% management fee, and the 50/50 split — so every proposal shows exactly what each party earns before a job is accepted.

Strategy 02
BRRRR — Existing Property Acquisitions
Buying distressed properties, rehabbing with Jeff's crew, placing tenants, and refinancing to recycle capital into the next deal.

Overview

The BRRRR method — Buy, Rehab, Rent, Refinance, Repeat — applied to existing distressed properties is Ironwood's primary portfolio-building strategy alongside new construction. Where new construction builds equity through the build process, existing BRRRR creates equity by purchasing below market value and forcing appreciation through renovation. The two strategies complement each other: new construction is slower but produces higher-quality assets; existing BRRRR is faster to execute and allows for opportunistic deal-by-deal acquisitions.

The BRRRR Loop

Buy distressed at 65–70% ARV → Rehab with Jeff's crew → Rent to qualified tenant → Refinance at ARV → Pull capital out → Repeat. Each cycle ideally returns all or most of the invested capital, leaving a cash-flowing asset in the portfolio with zero or minimal net capital deployed.

The MAO Formula

Maximum Allowable Offer (MAO) is the most important number in any BRRRR deal. Ironwood never pays more than 70% of ARV minus rehab costs. The MAO Calculator tool handles this automatically, but understanding the formula is non-negotiable.

Deal Analysis — What Makes a Good BRRRR

  • Purchase at or below MAO: No exceptions. A deal that requires paying above MAO is not a BRRRR deal — it is a gamble.
  • Rehab cost predictability: Jeff walks every property before an offer is made. His estimate is the rehab budget. No surprises = no margin erosion.
  • Rent coverage: Monthly rent must cover the DSCR mortgage payment at a 1.25 ratio minimum. At 6.5% on a $150,000 DSCR loan, the monthly payment is ~$950. Monthly rent must be $1,188 or above. Target $1,400+ per unit for comfortable coverage.
  • Market rent validation: Jordan pulls comparable rental listings before any offer is submitted. Never assume — verify what the market will actually pay.
  • Exit clarity: Before making an offer, Ty identifies the DSCR lender who will refinance this specific property type in this zip code. Not all DSCR lenders lend in all markets or on all property types.

Rehab Execution

Jeff manages the rehab with Ironwood's subcontractor network. BRRRR rehabs are not luxury renovations — they are strategic value-adds. The goal is to bring the property to rentable condition at the lowest possible cost while maximizing the appraisal value. This means focusing on items that appraisers and tenants care about: kitchens, bathrooms, flooring, paint, and curb appeal. Every dollar spent on a BRRRR rehab must produce at least two dollars of ARV increase.

High ROI Items

  • LVP flooring throughout
  • Kitchen cabinet repaint + hardware
  • Bathroom vanity + fixtures
  • Fresh interior paint
  • Landscaping / curb appeal
  • New light fixtures

Avoid Over-Improving

  • Custom cabinetry (use stock)
  • High-end appliances (mid-grade only)
  • Tile showers in C-class neighborhoods
  • Additions or structural changes
  • Any upgrade the market won't appraise

The Refinance Exit

The refinance is where the BRRRR strategy either works or fails. Ironwood targets DSCR lenders — Lima One Capital, Kiavi, Visio Lending, CoreVest Finance — who lend based on the property's income, not Ironwood's personal tax returns. DSCR lenders typically require three to six months of seasoning, a signed lease agreement, and a property that meets their minimum condition standards. Ty manages the refinance process; the target is to pull out 75% of ARV, pay off the hard money or purchase loan, and recycle the proceeds into the next deal.

Tenant Placement

Jordan markets every rental unit and manages tenant screening. Ironwood's screening criteria: credit score 620 or above, income at least three times monthly rent, no evictions in the last five years, verifiable rental history. Government housing vouchers (Section 8) are accepted on all qualifying units — government-guaranteed rent reduces vacancy risk and qualifies units for Ironwood Affordable Housing LLC programs. All leases are signed through Ironwood Property Management LLC, never directly through Ironwood LLC.

Strategy 03
New Construction BRRRR
Building duplexes every four months, placing tenants, refinancing at ARV, and recycling capital into the next build — indefinitely.

Overview

New construction BRRRR is Ironwood's highest-equity, highest-control strategy. Rather than buying existing distressed properties and competing with other investors for deals, Ironwood builds new — controlling the product quality, the timeline, and the final value from the ground up. The four-month build cycle, combined with a DSCR cash-out refinance at completion, creates a self-funding loop: each duplex returns its construction capital through the refi, which funds the land for the next build.

Core Thesis

Build a duplex every four months. Refi each one at ARV. Recycle the proceeds. Hold every door. By month 24, Ironwood holds six rental doors generating $3,150/month in cash flow — with $315,000 in equity — without ever raising outside capital.

Strategy 04
Wholesale & Deal Sourcing
How Ironwood finds off-market deals before other investors — and how to turn every lead into either a flip, a BRRRR acquisition, or an assignment fee.

Overview

The best deals are never on the MLS. Distressed sellers — people facing foreclosure, probate, divorce, inherited properties, major repairs they can not afford — often sell below market because they need speed and certainty, not the highest price. Ironwood's deal sourcing strategy is built around finding these sellers before anyone else does, through direct outreach, digital marketing, and community relationships.

Every lead that comes in gets evaluated against two criteria: does it meet Ironwood's MAO for a BRRRR hold, or can it be assigned to another investor for a fee? Ironwood never lets a deal walk away just because it does not fit the current acquisition criteria — an assignment or wholesale fee of $5,000–$20,000 is pure profit with no rehab risk.

Lead Sources

Digital — Inbound

  • Seller Lead Form (Ironwood website): The primary digital intake point. Motivated sellers fill out the form describing their property and situation. Ty reviews every submission within 24 hours.
  • Google Ads + Facebook/Instagram Ads: Targeted to Atlanta metro ZIP codes with high distressed property concentrations. Ad copy focuses on "sell as-is, close in 14 days, no repairs needed."
  • YouTube content: Educational content about selling to investors positions Ty as the trusted local expert. Sellers who watch three videos before calling are warmer and more motivated than cold ad leads.

Direct Outreach

  • Driving for Dollars: Ty or a designated driver routes through target neighborhoods looking for visual distress signals — overgrown lawns, boarded windows, tarped roofs, code violation notices, deferred maintenance. Properties are logged in the CRM and direct mail is sent within 48 hours.
  • Direct Mail: Targeted mailings to absentee owners, pre-foreclosure lists, probate leads, and tax-delinquent properties. Mail sequence: initial letter → follow-up postcard at 30 days → final letter at 60 days. Response rate is typically 1–3% but these are highly motivated sellers.
  • Cold Outreach / SMS: Batch texting services (Launch Control, REISift) allow personalized texts to distressed property lists at scale. All responses route to Ty's CRM for follow-up.

Relationship-Based

  • Real estate agents: Agents with expired listings, pocket listings, and seller clients who can not wait for traditional closing timelines. Build a relationship with 5–10 high-volume Atlanta agents.
  • Probate attorneys and estate planners: Heirs who inherit properties often want to sell quickly without making repairs. A relationship with one or two probate attorneys produces consistent deal flow.
  • Property managers: Landlords who are burned out or have problem tenants are motivated sellers. Property managers know which landlords are struggling.
  • Wholesaler network: Other wholesalers bring deals to Ironwood in exchange for a co-wholesale fee. Ironwood reciprocates by sending deals that do not fit its criteria to trusted partners.

The Three-Option Framework

Every lead that comes in gets evaluated against three options. Ironwood never has one answer — it has three, and it picks the one that maximizes return given the available capital and pipeline capacity.

OptionWhen to UseExpected Return
Keep as BRRRRMeets MAO, strong rental market, fits portfolio strategy$1,000–$1,500/mo cash flow + equity

Wholesale / AssignDoes not meet MAO, capital is deployed, or deal needs speed$5,000–$20,000 assignment fee

CRM and Follow-Up

Most motivated sellers do not sell the first time they are contacted. A seller who says no in January may be ready in April. Ironwood maintains a CRM (GoHighLevel) with every lead, their situation, and a follow-up sequence. Leads that are not ready now are nurtured with monthly touchpoints — a text, a call, a mailer — until they are ready to act. The pipeline value of consistent follow-up is greater than any single acquisition.

The Rule

Every seller lead gets a response within 24 hours. Every follow-up is logged in the CRM. No lead dies without at least three attempts. Speed and consistency separate Ironwood from competitors who let leads go cold.

Strategy 05
Rental Portfolio Management
How Ironwood holds, manages, and grows its long-term rental portfolio through Ironwood Property Management LLC.

Overview

Every property Ironwood acquires through BRRRR or new construction is held in the portfolio indefinitely. The portfolio is the asset base — the collateral for future leverage, the source of monthly cash flow, and the foundation of long-term wealth. Properties are sold only by unanimous consent of both Members when the financial case is compelling. The default posture is always hold.

All rental operations are conducted through Ironwood Property Management LLC, a wholly-owned subsidiary of Ironwood LLC. This entity isolates liability, establishes a professional management brand, and creates the legal and operational structure to scale to unlimited units without adding complexity to the parent company.

Portfolio Philosophy

We do not sell doors — we add them. Every unit is a monthly income stream, a line of credit waiting to be drawn, and an inflation hedge that appreciates while the tenant pays down the mortgage. The portfolio compounds. Selling breaks the chain.

Lease Structure

  • Lease term: 12 months initial term, month-to-month thereafter with 60-day notice requirement.
  • Rent increases: Annual increase of 3–5% at lease renewal, communicated 60 days before renewal date. Never skip annual increases — falling behind market rent is difficult to recover from without tenant turnover.
  • Security deposit: One month's rent, held in a dedicated escrow account per Georgia landlord-tenant law.
  • Late fees: Rent is due on the 1st, late after the 5th. Late fee: $75 flat plus $10/day. Applied consistently — no exceptions without documented hardship.
  • Government vouchers (Section 8): Accepted on all qualifying units. Government-guaranteed rent eliminates default risk and qualifies properties for Ironwood Affordable Housing LLC programs.

Maintenance and Turnover

  • Preventive maintenance: Annual HVAC service, semi-annual gutter cleaning, biannual exterior inspection. Ironwood Services LLC handles lawn and cleaning for all portfolio properties.
  • 24-hour maintenance response: Emergency repairs (water, HVAC, electrical) get same-day response. Non-emergency requests get 48-hour acknowledgment and 5-business-day resolution target.
  • Unit turnover: When a unit turns, Ironwood Services LLC handles cleaning. Jeff's crew handles any rehab needed. Target: unit back on market within 10 business days of vacancy.
  • Turnover budget: Set aside $1,500–$3,000 per door per year for maintenance and turnover. This comes out of cash flow before distributions.

Portfolio Leverage Strategy

As the portfolio grows, the equity in each property becomes the raw material for acquiring more. Ironwood does not treat portfolio equity as net worth on paper — it treats it as deployable capital waiting to be activated. The approved leverage instruments are DSCR cash-out refinances, blanket mortgages covering multiple properties, portfolio lines of credit, and cross-collateralization agreements. No individual property is sold to fund a new acquisition — equity is extracted through refinancing, never disposition.

At five doors, Ironwood begins conversations with portfolio lenders (CoreVest, Visio, Arbor) about blanket financing. At ten doors, the portfolio qualifies for institutional-grade terms that significantly reduce the cost of capital on all future acquisitions.

Strategy 06
Peach to Palms — Vacation Rental Strategy
How Ironwood operates short-term rentals under the Peach to Palms brand — acquisition, furnishing, platform strategy, and operations.

Overview

Peach to Palms LLC is a wholly-owned subsidiary of Ironwood LLC operating under the vacation rental brand "Peach to Palms." It represents the short-term rental (STR) arm of the Ironwood portfolio — properties designed and operated for nightly and weekly guests rather than long-term tenants. STR revenue per door is typically two to four times higher than long-term rental income on the same property, with the trade-off of higher operating costs and more active management. Peach to Palms is launched in Phase 3 — after the BRRRR portfolio is established and capital is available for STR-optimized acquisitions.

All Peach to Palms revenue flows to Ironwood LLC as company income. Ty Jackson manages operations as a Member and operator — this is an Ironwood company activity, not personal income for Ty. The 25% gross vacation rental revenue fee and 3% platform host passthrough are Ironwood company expenses, not personal compensation.

Brand Position

Peach to Palms is Atlanta-inspired hospitality — warm, design-forward, locally rooted. Every property tells a story. The brand targets business travelers, weekend getaway guests, and relocating families who want a home experience over a hotel. Jordan's design eye is the brand differentiator.

Property Acquisition Criteria

  • Location: Atlanta metro neighborhoods with high STR demand — Midtown, Old Fourth Ward, Grant Park, Inman Park, Buckhead, airport corridor. Check local STR regulations before acquisition — some Atlanta neighborhoods have restrictions.
  • Property type: 2–4 bedroom homes or condos preferred. Duplexes can be run as two separate STR units or one unit STR and one unit long-term rental — hybrid income model.
  • Revenue projection: Target 60%+ annual occupancy at $150–$250/night average daily rate (ADR). At 200 nights/year × $175 ADR = $35,000 gross revenue. After platform fees (3%), operating costs, and mortgage, target $15,000–$20,000 net per property annually.
  • Conversion right: Every Peach to Palms property can be converted to a long-term rental at any time by Member agreement. No STR acquisition is a one-way door — every property has a clear long-term rental fallback.

Setup and Furnishing

Jordan Sarabia designs every Peach to Palms property. The design is the product — guests choose Peach to Palms over a comparable listing because the space is beautiful, functional, and photographed well. Each property receives a full furniture and decor package ($8,000–$15,000 depending on size), professional photography, and a digital guidebook. Setup cost is a one-time capital expense recouped within the first six months of operation at target occupancy.

Strategy 07
Business Credit Building
The step-by-step roadmap from DUNS number to $100,000+ in business credit lines — without a personal guarantee.

Overview

Business credit is Ironwood's financial infrastructure. Without it, every purchase is made with personal credit or cash — limiting growth to the speed of cash accumulation. With it, Ironwood can fund material purchases, equipment, and operating expenses on net 30–90 terms, preserving cash for acquisitions. Business credit that reports to D&B, Experian Business, and Equifax Business becomes a tradeline history that unlocks larger credit lines, better terms with suppliers, and eventually bank financing without personal guarantees.

The strategy is simple: open the right accounts in the right order, pay them consistently and early, and let the credit profile build itself over 12–18 months. There are no shortcuts — but there is a specific sequence that produces results faster than random account opening.

The Goal

$100,000+ in business credit lines within 18 months of LLC formation — zero personal guarantee, all in the Ironwood LLC EIN. This becomes the operating capital layer that never requires a cash call from Members.

Strategy 08
Investor Capital Raise
How Ironwood raises outside capital while keeping 100% operational control — through accredited investors and community crowdfunding.

Overview

As Ironwood's deal volume grows, the capital required to fund acquisitions and construction will exceed what the remodeling business and refi proceeds alone can supply. Ironwood's investor capital strategy solves this through two parallel tracks: a private placement for accredited investors on standard deals, and community crowdfunding for mission-driven projects. In both cases, Ironwood retains complete operational control. Investors come in through deal-specific Project LLCs — they never acquire any interest in Ironwood LLC itself.

The Non-Negotiable

Investors never enter Ironwood LLC. Every raise is through a deal-specific Project LLC with Ironwood as the sole Managing Member. Investors are Non-Managing Members with no voting rights on operations. Ironwood controls every decision from acquisition through disposition.

Track 1 — Accredited Investors (Reg D 506(c))

Regulation D Rule 506(c) allows Ironwood to advertise investment opportunities publicly — on Instagram, YouTube, Facebook, and the website — as long as all investors are verified accredited. Accredited investors are individuals earning $200,000+ per year or with $1,000,000+ in net worth. Verification is required before accepting capital. A Private Placement Memorandum (PPM) prepared by a Georgia securities attorney is required before the first offering.

  • Minimum investment: $5,000 per deal
  • Preferred return: 8–10% annualized
  • Profit split above pref: 70% investors / 30% Ironwood promote
  • Ironwood management fee: 1–2% of capital raised or 5% of gross project revenue
  • Investor voting rights: None on operations. Limited consent on sale below contributed capital.
  • Best for: BRRRR acquisitions, flips, new construction projects with clear exit timelines

Track 2 — Community Crowdfunding (Reg CF)

Regulation Crowdfunding allows Ironwood to raise up to $5,000,000 per year from the general public — not just accredited investors — through SEC-registered portals like Wefunder, Republic, or StartEngine. This track is reserved for community-focused projects: veteran housing, affordable housing, and neighborhood revitalization. Minimum investment as low as $100. The raise itself becomes content — every update, every milestone, every before-and-after is a social media post that builds the Ironwood brand while simultaneously funding the deal.

  • Eligible projects: Ironwood Veteran Housing LLC, Ironwood Affordable Housing LLC, and neighborhood revitalization projects
  • Platform: Wefunder, Republic, or StartEngine (SEC-registered)
  • Minimum investment: $100
  • Returns: Modest preferred return (4–6%) or revenue share — the community mission is part of the value
  • Give-back option: By unanimous Member consent, Ironwood can donate its promote on community deals back to the project or a nonprofit partner

Commercial Real Estate — Target Asset Classes

NNN (Triple Net) Leases

In a NNN lease, the tenant pays rent plus all property taxes, insurance, and maintenance. The landlord collects a check and does nothing else. Gas stations, Dollar General, AutoZone, fast food pad sites, car washes, and dollar stores are all common NNN tenants. These are the most passive real estate assets that exist. Cap rates typically run 5–7%, and leases run 10–20 years with built-in rent escalations. This is what institutional money holds in massive volume — and it is accessible to Ironwood at the $500K–$2M per-asset level before scaling to larger deals.

Car Wash Acquisitions

Ironwood has an operational edge here that most real estate operators do not. Understanding the express wash model — membership-based recurring revenue, equipment depreciation schedules, site throughput, and chemical costs — means Ironwood can underwrite a car wash acquisition with confidence, not speculation. The real estate strategy is to acquire the land and building, and either operate the wash directly through a subsidiary or lease it to an established operator on a NNN basis.

Operate Directly

  • Higher income ceiling — keep 100% of net after expenses
  • Leverage existing operational expertise
  • Ironwood Car Wash LLC as subsidiary
  • Target: membership model, $35K–$60K net/mo per location
  • Best for first 1–2 locations while building track record

NNN Lease to Operator

  • Fully passive — tenant handles all operations
  • Easier to scale — no staffing or equipment management
  • 6–8% cap rate on stabilized car wash real estate
  • Long-term lease (10–15 yr) with rent escalations
  • Best for Phase 3+ when portfolio is larger

Gas Stations

The business model: buy the real estate (land + canopy + building), lease it to a fuel operator (Shell, BP, independent). You never touch fuel, licenses, or daily operations. The tenant pays NNN rent plus a fuel volume override in many structures. Cap rates 5–7%, leases 15–20 years, extremely stable. A single gas station pad in a strong Atlanta traffic corridor can generate $8,000–$15,000/mo in NNN rent with zero landlord responsibilities. This is the kind of asset that runs itself for decades.

Strip Mall / Flex Commercial

Acquire a 3–8 bay strip center anchored by a recession-proof tenant (dollar store, nail salon, tax service, Title Max). Fill remaining bays with local service businesses. Net lease or gross lease depending on tenant quality. Atlanta's suburban submarkets — South Fulton, Clayton County, DeKalb, Gwinnett — have strong demand for small-format neighborhood retail and are significantly more affordable to enter than Buckhead or Midtown. A $1.2M acquisition at a 7.5% cap rate generates $90K/year in gross income before financing.

Infrastructure Layers on Existing Real Estate

Once Ironwood holds real estate, infrastructure income can be layered on top with no additional acquisition cost.

  • Cell towers: AT&T, T-Mobile, and Verizon pay $1,500–$4,000/mo for rooftop or ground lease rights. Leases run 20–30 years with 3% annual escalations. If you own land or a commercial building in a coverage gap area, this income is essentially free.
  • EV charging stations: ChargePoint, Blink, and EVgo pay for access to high-traffic parking areas. Revenue share or flat lease. Strong demand in Atlanta metro. Also qualifies for federal tax credits under the Inflation Reduction Act.
  • Fiber conduit: Telecom companies pay for underground conduit access on commercial properties in growth corridors. One-time payment or long-term lease.
  • Billboard / signage: High-traffic commercial property can generate $500–$3,000/mo from a billboard ground lease with Lamar, Clear Channel, or an independent operator.

REIT Election — The $100M Exit Ramp

When Ironwood's portfolio reaches 100+ residential doors or a significant commercial portfolio (both are viable paths), a Real Estate Investment Trust (REIT) election becomes a structural option worth serious consideration. A REIT must distribute 90% of taxable income to shareholders as dividends, which eliminates corporate income tax. In exchange, the REIT can raise equity from the general public — not just accredited investors — through a public offering or a non-traded REIT structure.

  • Non-traded REIT: Ironwood raises capital from retail investors without going public. Minimum investment typically $1K–$5K. Platforms like Fundrise use this structure. This is realistic at $50M+ AUM.
  • Publicly traded REIT: Full SEC registration, quarterly earnings reporting, public market liquidity for investors. This is the institutional end game — not a near-term objective, but a 10-year horizon target that shapes decisions made today.
  • UpREIT structure: Allows property owners to contribute assets to the REIT in exchange for operating partnership units — a tax-deferred transaction. This is how Ironwood could consolidate subsidiary LLC holdings into a single REIT vehicle without triggering capital gains on appreciated assets.

Risk Factors

Securities Compliance

Raising capital from investors is a heavily regulated activity. The Reg D and Reg CF frameworks provide exemptions but they are not optional guidelines — they are legal requirements. Never accept investor capital before a PPM is in place and a securities attorney has reviewed the offering structure. One mistake creates personal liability for the managers.

Track Record Gap

Institutional investors will not commit capital without 3–5 years of audited returns. Trying to skip Phase 1 and Phase 2 to raise Fund II money will fail. The track record is not optional — it is the product. Do Phase 1 right, document everything, and the capital conversations become easy.

Commercial Underwriting Discipline

Commercial real estate (gas stations, car washes, strip malls) requires different underwriting than residential BRRRR. Cap rates, NOI, tenant credit quality, lease term, and environmental liability (especially for gas stations) all matter. Never acquire commercial real estate without a Phase I environmental study and a commercial real estate attorney reviewing the lease.

Opportunity Zone Regulatory Risk

OZ tax benefits are governed by IRS regulations that have been updated multiple times since the 2017 Tax Cuts and Jobs Act created the program. Congress could modify or eliminate the benefits. All OZ fund documents must disclose this risk. Build the deal to work even without the tax benefit — the tax treatment is upside, not the foundation.

Scaling Too Fast

Moving from residential to commercial to funds before the infrastructure is ready — auditors, attorneys, investor portal, compliance — creates exposure. Each phase has prerequisites. Complete them before moving to the next. A missed step at $10M AUM is manageable. The same mistake at $50M AUM is a legal and reputational event.
